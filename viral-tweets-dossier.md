## 📣 Amplifiers (who does the spreading)

Two distinct routes into virality:
- **82-0 route — athletes + beat writers + vertical brands.** One star (Haliburton) → beat writers (Goold) → media brands with big coordinated followings (Sleeper's regional accounts, RotoWire, HoopsHype, Barstool).
- **GeoSports route — a single big-creator plug + a newsletter.** **@JoePompliano** (sports-business creator, ~1M+): *"This is an awesome game. Highly recommend checking it out."* → a Yahoo Sports (Kendall Baker) newsletter mention → *"it exploded on Twitter"* (per [Sportico](https://www.sportico.com/business/tech/2026/geosports-trivia-game-creator-viral-wordle-1234901573/)). Hit 1M users in under a month.

## 🎯 Playbook for Pitching Lab

1. **Ship the share loop — highest ROI by far.** On a finished career / high OVR,
   hand the player a one-tap tweet pre-filled like:
   *"I built a `<OVR>` OVR pitcher who went to the Hall of Fame on Pitching Lab. Can you beat it? `<link>` #PitchingLab"*
   Dozens copied 82-0's *"I just went 82-0…"* verbatim — the flex is the funnel.
   You have the posting API (@pitchergami), but this needs **no** API at all —
   a `twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=…&url=…&hashtags=…` link works on Free tier.
2. **A branded hashtag** (82-0 had **#82and0**). Pick one (#PitchingLab / #162and0)
   and bake it into the share text so results form a browsable rail.
3. **Announcement voice: credit + twist.** The clones that shouted out 82-0
   (@TheMetsX, @NoahBairCuse) rode the wave. Your @pitchergami "Batter version is
   now live!" is already this — lean harder into "the perfect-season game, but you
   *build one player and sim their whole career*" as the differentiator.
4. **Engineer the "wait, THAT'S a Hall of Famer?" argument.** 82-0's virality was
   *debate* — surprising/unfair results people QT to argue about. Make the sim spit
   out screenshotable "how is this a first-ballot HOFer?" verdicts.
5. **Seed a launch list.** MLB beat writers (Goold-types), baseball meme accounts,
   and analytics brands (@AnalyticsSaidSo, RotoWire-equivalents). One well-placed
   creator plug (the Pompliano/GeoSports model) can be the whole ballgame.
6. **Tie to real MLB moments.** @pitchergami already posts "never happened in MLB
   history" stat lines — that's the same instinct as your "Last Night's Studs" hook.
   Pair a real-game moment with a "build this guy in Pitching Lab" CTA.
